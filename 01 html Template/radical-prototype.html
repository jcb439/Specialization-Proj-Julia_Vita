RADICAL PROTOTYPE
Julia Beitel & Vita Sipayung
How might we…

...recognize genuine environmental care through everyday actions, not just words?

...reframe material practices—repairing, gardening, swapping, sharing—as civic participation equal to voting or protesting?

...surface contradictions between what people say and what they do, in ways that invite reflection rather than blame?

...build infrastructures where communities see themselves not as consumers, but as active participants shaping the future?

Radical Themes:

• Recognition (care in action)

• Reframing (everyday practices as political)

• Reflection (contradictions without shame)

• Infrastructure (bigger system for participation)

Expanding Directions:

→ Prototype a system that measures environmental participation through repair, reuse, sharing, and redistribution at community scale.

→ A platform that documents environmental gestures that often go unnoticed (fixing, composting, cycling, lending tools).

→ Prototype a digital infrastructure where practice outweighs slogans — mapping how communities act on their values (what are the learnings from individual actions?).

→ Prototype a registry that treats material practices (gardening, repairing, swapping) as political acts equal to voting or protesting.

→ Prototype a tool that reveals gaps between what people/organizations say and what they do environmentally — but without judgment, as a space for reflection.
