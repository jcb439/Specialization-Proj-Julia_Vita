Key Texts Shaping Our Understanding

Core Research

Our research spans behavioral psychology, sustainable fashion studies, and consumer behavior to understand impulse drivers and interventions.

Textile Science and Clothing Technology
Fast Fashion, Fashion Brands and Sustainable Consumption
Editor:
Subramanian Senthilkannan Muthu
Springer

Key Insights

  • 52 micro-collections/year → constant obsolescence
  • 1 in 10 women wear garments only 3 times
  • Hedonistic motivations: shopping as entertainment
  • Social media amplifies non‑repetition pressures
"Fast fashion makes latest styles accessible at cheaper prices, pushing novelty over durability."
Sustainability in Textiles
ECO‑FRIENDLY AND FAIR
Fast Fashion and Consumer Behaviour
Edited by
Carolin Becker‑Leifhold & Mark Heuer
R

Historical & Systemic Analysis

  • Pre‑1980s: 2–4 collections; product‑driven
  • Fast fashion exploits identity fluidity & FOMO
  • Flexible supply chains enable disposability
  • Reforms: circularity, eco‑materials, policy
"Unsustainable across the lifecycle; externalizes costs."
The Sustainable Fashion Quest
Innovations in Business and Policy
NIKOLAY ANGUELOV
Routledge
Taylor & Francis Group

Consumer Behavior Reality

  • Style & price trump ecological factors
  • "Buy fewer, better" fails when cheap exists
  • Consumers rarely choose pricier alternatives
  • Industry must seed new sustainable trends
"Style and price, not ecology, drive purchases."
How to Break Up with Fast Fashion
Lauren Bravo

Psychological Interventions

  • Retail therapy exploits stress coping
  • Paradox of choice → fatigue
  • Wardrobe auditing first
  • Waiting periods curb impulses
  • Ask: "Do I need this? How often will I wear it?"
"Mind the triggers to make mindful choices."
