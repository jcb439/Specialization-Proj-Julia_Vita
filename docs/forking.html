Understanding the System

The highlighted path (grey nodes) traces from the fashion industry through manufacturing and overproduction to conscious purchasing as the key everyday practice. This is where our "speed bump" intervention operates.

Our intervention lenses are reflection and recognition—asking users to reflect on what they own and recognize their existing wardrobe value.

The behavioral incentives we activate are intrinsic motivation (satisfaction with what you have) and social pressure (reducing overconsumption), rather than external rewards.

Key Insight

While the fashion industry creates overproduction at the supply level, our intervention addresses it at the demand level—the moment of conscious purchasing. By introducing reflection at this critical point, we activate intrinsic motivation rather than relying on external incentives that may not scale or sustain behavior change.
