Barriers to Entry

Understanding Implementation Challenges

While our intervention addresses a critical gap in sustainable fashion technology, implementing a reflective shopping tool faces several key obstacles. Understanding these barriers helps us design solutions that are realistic, accessible, and sustainable.

Click on each barrier to learn more about its impact on our tool.

Time & Effort

+

Climate-friendly practices are often perceived as extra work, making it harder for people to integrate them into daily routines.

Impact on our tool: Users may resist adding steps to their shopping flow, even brief ones. The intervention must feel effortless and seamlessly integrated.

Cost

+

Sustainable fashion and eco-practices can be more expensive upfront, creating a financial barrier for many individuals.

Impact on our tool: While our tool is free, it challenges users to reconsider purchases, which may conflict with the appeal of affordable fast fashion.

Privacy & Trust

+

Users may be reluctant to have their actions tracked or shared, especially if transparency and data use are unclear.

Impact on our tool: Wardrobe tracking requires user data. Building trust through transparency about data usage and storage is essential.

Accessibility

+

Not everyone has equal access to repair shops, swaps, or affordable sustainable options in their communities.

Impact on our tool: Our intervention works best when sustainable alternatives exist. The tool must acknowledge varying access to resources.

Market Saturation

+

The eco-app space is crowded, and new initiatives risk blending in unless they offer clear differentiation or added value.

Impact on our tool: We must clearly communicate our unique value: intervention at the moment of purchase, not just awareness or alternatives.

Scalability

+

Community-based partners may lack resources to scale, and users may disengage if the platform feels like "just another app."

Impact on our tool: Maintaining user engagement over time requires ongoing value delivery and avoiding intervention fatigue.

Our Strategic Responses

Minimize Friction

Design the intervention to take seconds, not minutes. A brief pause is enough to trigger reflection.

Transparent Data Practices

Clearly communicate what data is collected, how it's used, and give users control over their information.

Focus on Behavior, Not Shame

Frame the tool as empowering conscious choice rather than guilting users about sustainability.

Unique Value Proposition

Our point-of-purchase intervention fills a gap that other tools miss: the critical moment between desire and decision.
